the framework

Every pitch must answer three questions.
For each of three things.

The problem

Three conditions that make this problem worth solving. Not symptoms. Structural conditions that guarantee this problem persists without intervention.

1. The pain is specific and undeniable
2. The market is large enough to matter
3. The timing is now, not someday
The solution

Three structural advantages that make this company the one that wins. Not features. Moats. The compounding advantages that get wider with every passing quarter.

1. A distribution edge nobody else has
2. A data or network effect moat
3. A switching cost that compounds
The path

Three paths to market dominance. Three ways this becomes defensible, durable, and irreplaceable. Not scenarios. Committed strategies with clear first steps.

1. The beachhead: one customer, done perfectly
2. The expansion: the second market
3. The inevitable: why this becomes infrastructure

Pitch in threes.

Three sentences on the problem. Three on the advantage. Three on the path. If you can't do it in nine sentences, keep refining until you can.

We have your pitch. If it passes the framework, you'll hear back within a week.
